# Universal Laser Slot Resizer & Visualizer Pro

A high-precision, client-side web tool designed to automatically detect, preview, and resize interlocking finger-joints and slots in SVG files for laser cutting. 

Whether your file was generated by **MakerCase**, **MateriForm**, or **CorelDRAW**, this tool automatically adapts to varying scale variables, strips away decorative vectors from calculation loops, and allows real-time interactive node editing.

## ✨ Key Features

* **Axis-Aligned Auto-Probing:** Automatically scans your vector strings to locate structural box joints based on your target material baseline.
* **Decoupled Browser CTM Viewport:** Utilizes native browser matrix transformations (`getScreenCTM`) to map coordinate spaces seamlessly, eliminating misalignment bugs caused by nested transformation groups (`matrix(...)`).
* **Interactive Selection Mode:** Single left-click directly on the preview map to manually toggle selection markers—instantly remove false positives on decorative accents or force-add missed slots.
* **Infinite Pan & Zoom Workspace:** Features fluid mouse-wheel zoom-to-cursor tracking and drag-to-pan capabilities for inspecting large, intricate templates.
* **Dual-Contrast Theme Bypass Engine:** Quickly switch between Dark and Light background modes with forced high-visibility vector stroke overrides to bypass internal hardcoded SVG style sheets.
* **Pure Client-Side Processing:** No servers, no data tracking. All XML construction parsing happens safely directly inside your browser.

## 🛠️ How It Works (The Math)

SVG files exported from different vector suites handle coordinate scaling differently:
* **CorelDRAW** often outputs coordinates multiplied by a scale factor of **100** ($3.00\text{mm} \to 300$).
* **MakerCase** outputs a raw $1:1$ millimeter scale ratio ($3.00\text{mm} \to 3$).# LaserCutResize

[Uploaded SVG] ➔ [ViewBox Ratio Calibration Engine] ➔ Automatically detects scale factor (x1 or x100)
│
[90° Orthogonal Step Scanner] ➔ Filters out diagonal/curved artwork ◄┘
│
[Inverse CTM Coordinate Transform] ➔ Syncs mouse pixels with internal XML nodes perfectly

The tool checks the ratio between the SVG's `viewBox` width and physical width attributes on upload to configure its scaling engine natively. It then targets pure horizontal and vertical paths ($l$ and $L$) matching the thickness matrix, completely ignoring complex cubic Bézier curves ($c$ and $C$).

## 📖 User Guide

### 1. Upload & Calibration
1. Upload your laser-ready SVG file.
2. Enter the **Expected Thickness** that the file was originally drawn for (e.g., `3.00` mm).
3. Click **Auto-Scan Slots**. The tool will isolate the material joints and highlight them with blue glowing anchor pins.

### 2. Manual Tweaking (If Needed)
* **To Pan:** Click and drag your mouse over an empty space in the workspace area.
* **To Zoom:** Use your mouse wheel or the floating HUD buttons ($+$, $-$, $\circlearrowleft$) in the top right.
* **To Deselect Decor:** Left-click an active blue circle sitting on an ornamental engraving line to clear it.
* **To Force a Slot:** Left-click on the center of any missed straight slot line to drop a manual override anchor point.

### 3. Resize & Export
1. Enter your measured **Actual Material Thickness** from your digital calipers.
2. Enter your laser cutter's custom beam width offset under **Laser Kerf** (subtracted dynamically to maintain tighter interlocking friction joints).
3. Click **Apply Selection**, verify the canvas geometry update, and click **Download Repaired SVG**.
